Modelling transmissible cancer

Student(s): Falter, F., Linders, L., Ludwig, D., Raff, T., Salam, J.; Supervisor(s): Katerina Stanková and Frank Thuijsman;

Problem Statement and Motivation:

The research questions that will be addressed during this project are as follow:
  1. What types of models, and ways of mathematically modelling exist to model DFTD1 and DFTD2 on a population of Tasmanian Devils?
  2. Can a mathematical model, when combined with spatial data, be used to make a spatial, agent-based, representation of the spread of DFTD1 and DFTD2?
  3. Can a trade-off be found between complexity and comprehensibility in the spatial model, such that the impact of the tweaking of specific variables can still be found?
  4. How complex does an agent-based spatial model have to be, in order to be an accurate representation of a population of Tasmanian Devils?
